Privacy Policy

Last updated: 9 June 2026

The short version: Aloud reads your PDFs entirely inside your own browser. Your files are never uploaded to us or anyone else. We don’t ask for your name or email, we don’t use tracking cookies, and we only count anonymous usage so we can keep the tool working.

1. Who we are

Aloud (“we”, “us”) is a free tool that reads PDF documents aloud in your web browser. This policy explains what happens to data when you use it. If you have any questions, contact us at [TODO: your-email@example.com].

2. Your PDFs never leave your device

When you open a PDF in Aloud, the file is read and converted to speech locally, on your own device. It is not sent to our servers or any third party. We never see, store, or have access to the documents you open. When you close or refresh the page, the file is gone from the app.

3. What we collect — and what we don’t

We do not collect:

  • Your name, email address, or any account details (there are no accounts).
  • The contents of your PDFs.
  • Anything that personally identifies you.

We collect a small amount of anonymous, aggregated usage information so we can understand whether the tool is working and worth maintaining — for example: how many people visit, which country region (not your exact location), what type of device, and which features get used (such as “a PDF was opened” or “play was pressed”). None of this identifies you and none of it includes your documents.

5. Cookies

Aloud does not set tracking or advertising cookies. We may use minimal local storage strictly to remember your own settings (such as your chosen voice or speed) on your device — this never leaves your browser. If this ever changes, we will update this policy and, where required, ask for your consent.

6. Tips and donations

Aloud is free. If you choose to leave a voluntary tip to help with running costs, the payment is handled by a third-party platform [TODO: e.g. Ko-fi / Buy Me a Coffee / PayPal]. 7. Server logs

Like virtually all websites, our hosting provider may automatically record basic technical information (such as IP addresses and browser type) for short periods to keep the service secure and reliable. This is standard infrastructure logging and is not used to identify or track you.

8. Children

Aloud is a general-purpose tool and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any personal data from children.

9. Your rights

Depending on where you live (for example under UK/EU GDPR or California’s CCPA), you have rights over your personal data, such as access and erasure. Because Aloud does not collect personal data that identifies you, there is normally nothing for us to hold or delete — but if you have any concern, contact us at [TODO: your-email@example.com] and we’ll help.

10. International visitors

Aloud can be used from anywhere. Because processing happens in your own browser, your documents stay in your country, on your device.
